What is a TENS Unit and Does It Work?

A TENS unit is a portable, battery-powered device that delivers mild electrical impulses through adhesive electrodes to the skin. These signals reduce pain transmission and trigger endorphin release, offering relief for chronic or acute pain.

TENS therapy is commonly used to manage hyperalgesia and can be used at home or in clinical settings. Discover its versatile uses and evidence-backed effectiveness in pain management.

Benefits of TENS Units

TENS offers noninvasive, drug-free pain relief — perfect for avoiding dependency on medications. It's compact, travel-friendly, and effective for many types of pain including menstrual, joint, and post-surgery discomfort.

What Is TENS Therapy Used to Treat?

  • Osteoarthritis
  • Fibromyalgia
  • Tendinitis
  • Bursitis
  • Diabetic Neuropathy
  • Peripheral Artery Disease

How Does TENS Therapy Work?

TENS therapy sends a gentle electrical current through skin electrodes. This current blocks pain signals from reaching the brain and stimulates endorphin release. The result? Fast, reliable pain relief without medication.
